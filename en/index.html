How does the allowance work?

For the year 2025:

  • Up to 20 km: €0.30 / km.
  • From the 21st km: €0.38 / km.
  • Maximum limit: €4,500 (does not apply when using your own car).

For the year 2026 (New):

  • Starting in 2026, the commuter allowance is a flat €0.38 per kilometer for your one-way distance.
  • The previous €0.30 tier for the first 20 km no longer applies.

Optimizing Your Income Tax Return with Pendlerpauschale

When filing your annual income tax return (Einkommensteuererklärung) in Germany, understanding the mechanics of the commuter allowance (Pendlerpauschale) is vital for reducing your overall taxable income. This deduction serves as an essential component of your total income-related expenses, effectively lowering your overall tax burden based on how far you travel to work.

Understanding Tax Deductible Income-Related Expenses

Every employee is eligible to claim travel costs as part of their standard yearly income-related expenses (Werbungskosten). The German tax system applies the standard lump-sum allowance automatically, but if your calculation exceeds this base threshold, tracking your daily travel distance accurately will ensure a higher tax refund.

The Impact of Distance and Travel Method

The structural deduction depends entirely on the one-way distance between your primary residence and your place of work. Whether you use a passenger car, public transit, or pool with coworkers, maximizing your write-offs depends directly on keeping track of legal changes—such as the streamlined flat rates introduced for the 2026 tax year.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is there a maximum limit?

Yes, a maximum of €4,500/year applies if you use public transport or a bicycle. This cap does not apply if you commute using your own car.

What about Home Office days?

The home office allowance (Home-Office-Pauschale) applies to remote working days, meaning you cannot claim the commuter allowance for the same day.

How does a carpool affect the allowance?

Every participant in a carpool can claim the distance flat rate based on their respective one-way commute distance. However, for passengers, the yearly cap is limited to €4,500.

Can I write off actual fuel costs instead?

For regular commutes between home and your primary workplace, you must use the uniform distance flat rate. Tracking actual vehicle costs is typically reserved for corporate business trips.
