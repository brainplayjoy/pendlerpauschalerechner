How does the allowance work?

For the year 2025:

  • Up to 20 km: €0.30 / km.
  • From the 21st km: €0.38 / km.
  • Maximum limit: €4,500 (does not apply when using your own car).

For the year 2026 (New):

  • Starting in 2026, the commuter allowance is a flat €0.38 per kilometer for your one-way distance.
  • The previous €0.30 tier for the first 20 km no longer applies.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is there a maximum limit?

Yes, a maximum of €4,500/year, unless you use your own car.

What about Home Office?

The home office allowance applies here, not the commuter allowance.

How does the commuter allowance work?

For the year 2025:

  • For the first 20 kilometers: €0.30 per kilometer.
  • From the 21st kilometer: Increased rate of €0.38 per kilometer.
  • Important: If you use public transport (bus, train, bicycle, etc.), the allowance is limited to €4,500 per year. This limit does not apply for trips with your own car.

For the year 2026 (New):

  • Starting in 2026, the commuter allowance is a flat €0.38 per kilometer for your one-way distance.
  • The previous €0.30 tier for the first 20 km no longer applies.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does the allowance apply to both the outward and return journey?

No, the commuter allowance applies exclusively to the one-way distance (one-way only).

What happens on home office days?

No commuter allowance can be claimed for home office days. Use the home office allowance (Home-Office-Pauschale) for these days.
