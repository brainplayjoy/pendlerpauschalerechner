Optimize your Income Tax Return with the Homeoffice Allowance

When filing your annual income tax return in Germany, it is crucial to correctly state the Homeoffice Allowance (flat rate) to reduce your taxable income. This deduction is an essential part of your income-related expenses (Werbungskosten) and relieves all employees who work from home temporarily or completely.

Important Note about Homeoffice Allowance

The Homeoffice Allowance is not paid by the employer. It is a tax deduction claimed in the German income tax return. It also falls within the general employee expense (Werbungskosten) framework. Therefore, this calculator clearly calculates the deductible amount, not the actual tax refund. The actual tax benefit depends on the taxpayer's individual tax situation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is there a maximum limit?

Yes, the flat rate is currently limited to 210 days. Since the rate is €6 per day, the absolute maximum limit is €1,260 per year (for 2020 to 2022, this was max. €600).

Do I need a closed-off study room?

No, contrary to the strict rules for the "home study room" (häusliches Arbeitszimmer), any workspace in your apartment is sufficient for this daily flat rate.

Can I combine commuting and home office?

Yes, in principle, but not for the same working day. You either claim the travel costs (Commuter Allowance) OR the Homeoffice Allowance for a specific day.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does the allowance also apply to working at the kitchen table?

Yes! Unlike the home study room (häusliches Arbeitszimmer), the Homeoffice Allowance is completely independent of the spatial conditions in your apartment.

Can I combine the Homeoffice Allowance and Commuter Allowance?

Yes, but not on the same day. If you work in the home office and go to the office on the same day, you may only claim the commuter allowance for the journey.

Do I have to prove the days to my employer?

You must make the days credible to the tax office. It is recommended to get a certificate from your employer stating how many days you were typically allowed to work in the home office.
