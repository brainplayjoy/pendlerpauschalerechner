German Commuter Allowance 2026: The New Flat Rate Guide

Moving to a new country comes with many financial adjustments. If you are an expat working in Germany, understanding the local tax system can save you thousands of Euros. One of the most valuable tax reliefs available to employees is the Commuter Allowance, known locally as the Pendlerpauschale.

The year 2026 has brought major updates to how this allowance is calculated. This comprehensive guide breaks down exactly how the new system works and how you can claim your money back.

What is the Commuter Allowance?

In Germany, the government allows you to deduct the cost of traveling between your home and your primary workplace from your taxable income. This deduction is designed to lower your overall income tax burden.

The Major 2026 Rule Change

As of 2026, the old tiered system has been completely abolished. Germany now uses a simple, unified flat rate. You get a flat €0.38 per kilometer starting from the very first kilometer of your commute.
